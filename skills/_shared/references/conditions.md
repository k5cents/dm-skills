# Conditions Reference
*Systems Reference Document 5.2.1 (CC BY 4.0) — 2024 mechanics*

A condition lasts until removed by an effect that specifies how.
Conditions do not stack — a second application has no additional effect.
Individual files in `rules-glossary/rules-definitions/<condition>.md` have full SRD text.

---

## Blinded
- Can't see; automatically fails ability checks requiring sight
- Attack rolls against the creature have Advantage; its attack rolls have Disadvantage

## Charmed
- Can't attack the charmer or target them with damaging abilities or magical effects
- The charmer has Advantage on ability checks to interact socially with the creature

## Deafened
- Can't hear; automatically fails ability checks requiring hearing

## Exhaustion
Cumulative levels 1–6; die at level 6.

| Per Level | Effect |
|---|---|
| Each level | D20 Test rolls reduced by 2 × Exhaustion level |
| Each level | Speed reduced by 5 feet × Exhaustion level |

Finishing a Long Rest removes 1 Exhaustion level.

## Frightened
- Disadvantage on ability checks and attack rolls while the source of fear is in line of sight
- Can't willingly move closer to the source of fear

## Grappled
- Speed becomes 0 and can't increase
- Disadvantage on attack rolls against any target other than the grappler
- Ends if the grappler is incapacitated or the creature is moved outside the grappler's reach

## Incapacitated
- Can't take actions, Bonus Actions, or Reactions
- Can't speak; Concentration is broken
- Disadvantage on Initiative rolls

## Invisible
- Can't be seen without special sense; counts as Heavily Obscured for Hiding
- Attack rolls against the creature have Disadvantage; its attack rolls have Advantage

## Paralyzed
- Has the Incapacitated condition; Speed 0
- Automatically fails Strength and Dexterity saving throws
- Attack rolls against have Advantage; any hit within 5 feet is a Critical Hit

## Petrified
- Transformed into inanimate substance; has the Incapacitated condition; Speed 0
- Unaware of surroundings; attack rolls against have Advantage
- Automatically fails Strength and Dexterity saving throws
- Resistance to all damage; Immunity to Poisoned condition

## Poisoned
- Disadvantage on attack rolls and ability checks

## Prone
- Movement options: crawl (costs extra foot per foot), or spend half Speed to stand (ends condition)
- Disadvantage on attack rolls
- Attack rolls against have Advantage if attacker is within 5 feet, otherwise Disadvantage

## Restrained
- Speed becomes 0 and can't increase
- Attack rolls against have Advantage; its attack rolls have Disadvantage
- Disadvantage on Dexterity saving throws

## Stunned
- Has the Incapacitated condition
- Automatically fails Strength and Dexterity saving throws
- Attack rolls against have Advantage

## Unconscious
- Has the Incapacitated and Prone conditions; drops whatever it's holding; Speed 0
- Unaware of surroundings
- Automatically fails Strength and Dexterity saving throws
- Attack rolls against have Advantage; any hit within 5 feet is a Critical Hit
- Remains Prone when condition ends

## Status Effects

### Bloodied
A creature is Bloodied when its Hit Points drop to half its Hit Point Maximum or lower.

### Concentration
Broken by: starting another Concentration effect; taking damage (Con save, DC = max of 10 or half damage taken, max DC 30); gaining the Incapacitated condition; dying.

### Surprised
A creature caught unawares at the start of combat has Disadvantage on its Initiative roll.
